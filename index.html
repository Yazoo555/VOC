Eye Examinations

Your Eyes Deserve
Expert Attention

The Process

What Happens During Your Eye Exam

01

Patient History & Consultation

We begin by reviewing your vision history, current concerns, medications and lifestyle factors that may affect your eye health.

02

Visual Acuity Testing

We measure how clearly you can see at various distances using standardised eye charts, assessing both near and far vision.

03

Refraction & Prescription

Using a phoropter and retinoscope, we precisely determine your refractive error and the exact prescription needed.

04

Ocular Health Assessment

A thorough examination of your eyes' internal and external structures to screen for glaucoma, cataracts and other conditions.

05

Results & Recommendations

We discuss your results clearly, explain any findings, and recommend the most appropriate care plan for your needs.

Vision Conditions

Common Conditions We Address

Nearsightedness

Myopia causes difficulty seeing distant objects clearly. Corrected with concave lenses or contact lenses.

Farsightedness

Hyperopia makes close objects appear blurry. Treated with convex prescription lenses.

Astigmatism

Irregular corneal shape causing blurred vision at all distances, corrected with cylindrical lenses.

Presbyopia

Age-related loss of near focus, addressed with reading glasses, bifocals or progressive lenses.

FAQ

Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything you need to know about our services and what to expect at Vision Optical.

How often should I get an eye exam? +
Adults with no vision problems should have a comprehensive eye exam every 1–2 years. If you wear glasses or contact lenses, have a family history of eye disease, or experience any vision changes, annual exams are recommended. Children should have their first eye exam at age 1 and regular follow-ups throughout school years.
Do you provide prescription glasses? +
Yes, absolutely. We offer a full range of prescription glasses including single-vision lenses for nearsightedness, farsightedness and astigmatism, as well as reading glasses, bifocals and progressive lenses. We carry a wide selection of frames and premium lens materials including high-index, polycarbonate and Trivex lenses with various coatings.
Are progressive lenses available? +
Yes, we specialise in progressive (no-line multifocal) lenses. These are ideal for patients over 40 who need correction for both near and far vision. Our progressive lenses are available in several designs to suit different lifestyles, including standard, enhanced and premium digital progressive options.
Do you offer contact lenses? +
Yes, we offer a full range of contact lenses including daily disposable lenses, monthly replacement lenses, toric lenses for astigmatism, multifocal contact lenses and more. A contact lens fitting consultation is included to ensure proper fit, comfort and vision correction.
How long does an eye examination take? +
A comprehensive eye examination at Vision Optical Center typically takes 30–45 minutes. This includes patient history review, visual acuity testing, refraction, and a thorough ocular health assessment. We recommend arriving 5–10 minutes early for new patient registration.
What are your operating hours? +
We are open Sunday through Friday from 10:30 AM to 7:30 PM, and on Saturdays from 8:00 AM to 10:00 AM. Walk-in patients are welcome, though we recommend booking an appointment for eye examinations. You can reach us at 986-0479291 or email visionoptical002@gmail.com.
Do children need eye exams? +
Yes, children's eye exams are very important. Undetected vision problems can significantly affect a child's learning and development. We recommend children have their first eye exam at around age 1, then again before starting school, and annually during school years.
